Rosalind Franklin

The Dark Lady of DNA 🧬

Rosalind Elsie Franklin was a British chemist and X-ray crystallographer. Her research was cruical in our understanding in the molecular structures of DNA and RNA. It helped reveal how DNA econdes and replicates genetic information which formed the basis of modern molecular biology, genetic enegineering and gene therapy. As a result of her work in DNA being largely unrecognized during her life, this page is written to give a brief overview about it.

Timeline

  • 1920: Rosalind Elsie Franklin was born in London, England
  • 1938: Franklin went to Newnham College and studied chemistry
  • 1945: She recieved a doctorate from Cambridge
  • 1947-1950: She worked at the State Chemical Laboratory in Paris studying X-ray diffraction technology
  • 1950: She joined the Biophysical Laboratory at King's College, London where she began to apply her knowledge on X-ray diffraction techniques onto DNA
  • 1952: Photo 51 was made. It is an x-ray diffication image that shows the double helix.
